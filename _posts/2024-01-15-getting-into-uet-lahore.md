Getting into the **University of Engineering and Technology (UET) Lahore** for Computer Engineering was one of the most defining moments of my life. It was the result of years of hard work, sleepless nights, and an unshakeable belief that I could make it.

---

## The Dream Begins

Ever since I was a child, I was fascinated by computers. I remember taking apart old radios and trying to figure out how they worked. When I got my first computer, I spent more time wondering *how* it worked than actually using it. That curiosity never left me.

By the time I reached my intermediate years, I knew one thing for certain — I wanted to study **Computer Engineering at UET Lahore**. Not just any university. UET. The best engineering university in Pakistan.

![Students in classroom]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/pexels-karola-g-7320375.jpg)
*The journey starts with a dream and a lot of hard work*

---

## The Preparation

The entry test for UET is no joke. The **ECAT (Engineering College Admission Test)** covers Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and English — and the competition is fierce. Thousands of students from across Punjab appear for a limited number of seats.

My preparation strategy was simple but brutal:

- **Wake up at 5 AM** every day for focused study
- **Past papers** — I solved at least 10 years of past ECAT papers
- **Mathematics first** — it carries the most weight in Computer Engineering
- **Weekly mock tests** to simulate real exam conditions
- **No social media** during the last two months of preparation

There were days I wanted to quit. Days when the syllabus felt endless and the seats felt impossibly few. But I kept going.

---

## The Exam Day

The morning of the ECAT, I woke up before my alarm. I remember sitting at the breakfast table, too nervous to eat, going over formulas in my head. My mother made me eat anyway — she always knew best.

The exam hall was packed. Hundreds of students, all with the same dream, all equally nervous. When the paper was placed in front of me, I took a deep breath and told myself:

> *"You have prepared for this. Trust the process."*

The Mathematics section went well. Physics was tricky but manageable. I finished with a few minutes to spare and reviewed my answers carefully.

![Focused student studying]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/pexels-polina-tankilevitch-6929185.jpg)
*Long hours of preparation finally paying off*

---

## The Wait

Waiting for the results was the hardest part. Two weeks of constantly refreshing the UET website, unable to focus on anything else. I kept second-guessing my answers, wondering if I had made mistakes.

Then one evening, I opened the results page and searched for my roll number.

**Merit list — Computer Engineering Department.**

My name was there.

I read it three times to make sure I was not dreaming. Then I called my parents.

---

## First Day at UET

Walking through the gates of UET Lahore as a student for the first time was surreal. The grand architecture, the massive campus, the energy of thousands of bright minds all gathered in one place — it was overwhelming in the best possible way.

![University life]({{site.baseurl}}/assets/img/pexels-tima-miroshnichenko-6549344.jpg)
*The energy of university life at UET Lahore*

The Computer Engineering department welcomed us with an orientation session. I looked around at my classmates — these were the people who had also fought hard to be here. I felt a deep sense of respect for every single one of them.

---

## What I Learned From This Journey

Getting into UET taught me lessons that go far beyond textbooks:

1. **Consistency beats talent** — Showing up every day matters more than occasional bursts of brilliance
2. **Your environment matters** — Surround yourself with people who push you forward
3. **Trust the process** — Hard work does not always show results immediately, but it always pays off eventually
4. **Family is everything** — My parents sacrificed so much to support my dream

---

## To Anyone Still on Their Journey

If you are currently preparing for ECAT or any competitive exam, I want you to know this — **it is possible**. The seats are limited but your potential is not. Work hard, stay consistent, and believe in yourself even when it is difficult.

UET Lahore was my dream. I fought for it and I got it.

Now I fight to make the most of it every single day.
